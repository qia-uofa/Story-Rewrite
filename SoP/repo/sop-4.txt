Harry wakes after Hagrid's arrival and realizes the news about Hogwarts was not a dream. Hagrid explains that Harry's parents left him money at Gringotts, the wizarding bank, and takes him to London. Along the way, Harry learns more about the hidden magical world, including the Ministry of Magic, goblins, and why magic is kept secret from Muggles.

They enter the Leaky Cauldron, where Harry is recognized and warmly greeted by many witches and wizards, including Professor Quirrell, who will teach Defense Against the Dark Arts. Hagrid then opens the entrance to Diagon Alley, a bustling wizard shopping street full of magical shops and supplies.

At Gringotts, Harry discovers a fortune left by his parents. Hagrid also retrieves a mysterious package from a high-security vault on Dumbledore's behalf. Afterward, Harry buys his Hogwarts supplies: robes, books, potion equipment, parchment, and other school items. He meets a pale, arrogant boy in Madam Malkin's robe shop who speaks proudly of Slytherin and looks down on Muggle-born students, making Harry uncomfortable.

Hagrid buys Harry a snowy owl as a birthday present, and they finish the day at Ollivanders, where Harry gets his wand. Mr. Ollivander reveals that Harry's wand shares a phoenix feather core with the wand that gave him his scar, making the match unusual and significant.

As Harry and Hagrid return to the ordinary world, Harry feels overwhelmed by his fame and by how much everyone expects from him despite his knowing almost nothing about magic. Hagrid reassures him that he will learn at Hogwarts and gives him his ticket for the journey from King's Cross on September first.
