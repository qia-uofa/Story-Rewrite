Harry wakes in the hut hoping Hagrid's arrival was not a dream, and discovers that it is real when an owl delivers the newspaper. Hagrid explains that they must go to London to buy Harry's school supplies and first visit Gringotts, the wizarding bank, where Harry's parents left him money.

They travel back to the mainland and then to London, where Hagrid leads Harry to the Leaky Cauldron. Inside, Harry is astonished when witches and wizards recognize him and treat him as famous. He also meets Professor Quirrell, who will teach Defense Against the Dark Arts at Hogwarts. Through a magical entrance behind the pub, Hagrid introduces Harry to Diagon Alley.

In Diagon Alley, Harry sees wizarding shops for the first time. At Gringotts, goblins take Harry and Hagrid deep underground. Harry discovers a vault full of money left by his parents, and Hagrid retrieves a mysterious package from vault 713 on Dumbledore's behalf.

Harry then buys his Hogwarts supplies. While being fitted for robes, he meets a pale, arrogant boy who boasts about wizarding families, Quidditch, and Slytherin, making Harry uncomfortable. Hagrid explains Quidditch and the Hogwarts houses, warning that many dark wizards came from Slytherin. Harry buys books, potion supplies, a cauldron, scales, a telescope, parchment, quills, and ink. As a birthday present, Hagrid buys him a snowy owl.

Finally, Harry visits Ollivanders to get his wand. After trying several, he is chosen by a holly and phoenix feather wand. Mr. Ollivander reveals that the phoenix feather in Harry's wand came from the same phoenix as the feather in Voldemort's wand, the one that gave Harry his scar. This unsettles Harry.

At the end of the day, Hagrid takes Harry to Paddington for his train back to the Dursleys. Harry feels overwhelmed because everyone expects great things from him, though he knows almost nothing about magic or his own past. Hagrid reassures him that he will learn at Hogwarts and gives him his ticket for September first.
