The chapter introduces the Dursleys, a proudly ordinary family living at number four, Privet Drive, who fear any connection to Mrs. Dursley’s strange sister and her family, the Potters. On an otherwise normal Tuesday, Mr. Dursley notices unsettling signs: owls flying in daylight, oddly dressed people whispering about the Potters, and reports of shooting stars across Britain.

That night, Albus Dumbledore arrives on Privet Drive and meets Professor McGonagall, who has been watching the Dursleys. They discuss the shocking news that Voldemort has disappeared after killing Lily and James Potter but failing to kill their infant son, Harry. Harry has survived with only a lightning-shaped scar, becoming famous in the wizarding world as the child who somehow defeated Voldemort.

Hagrid arrives on a flying motorcycle carrying baby Harry. Despite McGonagall’s doubts about leaving him with such unpleasant relatives, Dumbledore believes Harry should grow up away from fame until he is ready to understand who he is. Dumbledore leaves Harry on the Dursleys’ doorstep with a letter explaining everything. As the magical world secretly celebrates, Harry sleeps, unaware that he is famous as “the boy who lived.”
