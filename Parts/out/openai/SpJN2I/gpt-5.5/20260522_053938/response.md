Mr. and Mrs. Dursley dislike anything strange and avoid speaking of Mrs. Dursley's sister, Lily Potter, and her family. On an otherwise normal Tuesday, Mr. Dursley notices unusual events: a cat behaving oddly, people in cloaks gathering in public, owls flying in daylight, shooting stars, and whispers about the Potters and a boy named Harry.

Rubeus Hagrid arrives on a flying motorcycle carrying baby Harry. Although Professor McGonagall worries that the Dursleys are unsuitable guardians, Dumbledore insists Harry should grow up away from fame until he is ready to understand his past. Dumbledore leaves Harry on the Dursleys' doorstep with a letter explaining what happened. As the wizarding world celebrates Voldemort's fall and honors Harry as "the boy who lived," baby Harry sleeps, unaware of his fame or the difficult life awaiting him with the Dursleys.
