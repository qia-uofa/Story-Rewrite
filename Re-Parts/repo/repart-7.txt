“Am I really that famous?” Hughie asked, still staring after the way Starlight had said his name.
“Of course,” Starlight said. “If I had survived Homelander, I’d have read every file about it. Do you know your division yet? I’ve asked around. I want Guardian Red—proper rescue work, real front-line heroics—but Horizon Wing would be respectable too.”
She looked back at Gecko, who was searching beneath the seats for his escaped bio-toad. “Anyway, we should keep looking. You two ought to change soon; we must be close.”
Then she marched out with Gecko, leaving the compartment suddenly quieter.
Mother’s Milk let his shoulders drop. “Whatever division I get, I hope she isn’t in it. She’s all right, but she talks like she’s already passed the exams.”
“What does your family expect?” Hughie asked. “Guardian Red,” Mother’s Milk said, gloom returning. “Everyone in my family talks about it like destiny. Horizon Wing wouldn’t be awful. But Dominion Track? Ambition, command theory, old Viltrumite collaborators... Mum would never stop worrying.”
