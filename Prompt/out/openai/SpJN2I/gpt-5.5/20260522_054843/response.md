## Homelander

Homelander replaces the central godlike figure: adored by the public, feared by allies, and nearly impossible to challenge directly. His presence should dominate any scene where power, fame, intimidation, or moral corruption are central.

## Billy Butcher

Billy Butcher replaces the ruthless revenge-driven leader. He is charismatic, violent, manipulative, and willing to sacrifice almost anything to destroy supes and the systems protecting them.

## Hughie Campbell

Hughie Campbell replaces the ordinary protagonist pulled into extraordinary violence. He provides the emotional anchor, often torn between conscience, fear, loyalty, and the desire for justice.

## Starlight / Annie January

Starlight replaces the pure-hearted heroic idealist. She represents the conflict between genuine goodness and a corrupt superhero industry that commodifies morality.

## Queen Maeve

Queen Maeve replaces the jaded veteran champion. She has immense strength and public status but carries deep regret over years of compromise and complicity.

## A-Train

A-Train replaces the arrogant speed-based rival. He is defined by celebrity pressure, insecurity, addiction to power, and the consequences of careless violence.

## The Deep

The Deep replaces the insecure comic-relief supe with a darker edge. He is desperate for validation, easily manipulated, and constantly trying to reclaim status.

## Black Noir

Black Noir replaces the silent assassin or masked elite warrior. He communicates little but serves as a terrifying instrument of corporate violence.

## Mother's Milk

Mother's Milk replaces the disciplined tactical teammate. He is practical, organized, loyal, and driven by personal history with Vought and supes.

## Frenchie

Frenchie replaces the chaotic inventor, demolitions expert, or criminal specialist. He is emotionally intense, technically skilled, and haunted by past mistakes.

## Kimiko Miyashiro

Kimiko replaces the silent lethal protector. She is brutal in combat yet deeply human, defined by trauma, loyalty, and the struggle to choose her own identity.

## Stan Edgar

Stan Edgar replaces the calculating ruler behind the throne. He is calm, corporate, strategic, and more frightening because he rarely needs to raise his voice.

## Victoria Neuman

Victoria Neuman replaces the hidden political threat. She presents herself as a reformer while secretly possessing lethal power and concealed loyalties.

## Ashley Barrett

Ashley Barrett replaces the anxious bureaucratic handler. She manages image, crisis, scandal, and corporate survival while being trapped inside the machine she serves.

## Soldier Boy

Soldier Boy replaces the legendary old-world champion. He is a symbol of past glory, toxic masculinity, militarized celebrity, and destructive legacy.

## Grace Mallory

Grace Mallory replaces the retired spymaster or intelligence mentor. She knows the history of the conflict and understands the cost of fighting Vought.

## Madelyn Stillwell

Madelyn Stillwell replaces the corporate caretaker and manipulator. She understands how to manage powerful supes through affection, leverage, and public image.

## Stormfront

Stormfront replaces the charismatic extremist villain. She uses popularity, media, and ideology to conceal hatred and expand influence.

## Translucent

Translucent replaces the invisible infiltrator. His powers make him dangerous for surveillance, intimidation, and predatory control.

## Lamplighter

Lamplighter replaces the disgraced former champion. He is associated with fire, guilt, blackmail, and irreversible past atrocities.

## Mesmer

Mesmer replaces the psychic informant. His usefulness comes from reading minds, extracting secrets, and selling information to survive.

## Popclaw

Popclaw replaces the tragic fallen performer. She embodies the cost of exploitation, addiction, fading fame, and corporate neglect.

## Ryan Butcher

Ryan Butcher replaces the powerful child heir. He represents inherited power, innocence under pressure, and the possibility of becoming either savior or catastrophe.
