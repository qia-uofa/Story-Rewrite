## Part 1

The chapter introduces the Stillwell household, a proudly ordinary Vought-approved family living at number four, Privet Drive, who fear any connection to Madelyn Stillwell’s strange sister and her dangerous friends, the Butchers. On an otherwise normal Tuesday, Stan Edgar notices unsettling signs: surveillance drones circling in daylight, oddly dressed supes whispering about the Campbells, and reports of strange blasts of light across Britain.

That night, Grace Mallory arrives on Privet Drive and meets Queen Maeve, who has been watching the Stillwells. They discuss the shocking news that Homelander has disappeared after killing Becca and Billy Butcher but failing to kill their infant son, Hughie. Hughie has survived with only a lightning-shaped scar, becoming famous in the supe world as the child who somehow stood against Homelander.

Mother’s Milk arrives in a battered black van carrying baby Hughie. Despite Maeve’s doubts about leaving him with such unpleasant relatives, Mallory believes Hughie should grow up away from Vought’s fame machine until he is ready to understand who he is. Mallory leaves Hughie on the Stillwells’ doorstep with a letter explaining everything. As the supe world secretly celebrates, Hughie sleeps, unaware that he is famous as “the boy who walked away.”

## Part 2

Nearly ten years after being left with the Stillwells, Hughie Campbell lives neglected in a cupboard under the stairs while his spoiled cousin A-Train is showered with attention, sneakers, and gifts. On A-Train’s birthday, the family is forced to take Hughie along to the zoo when their usual babysitter is unavailable.

At the zoo, Hughie briefly enjoys himself, but things turn strange in the aquatic house when he realizes he can communicate with Timothy, a trapped octopus. After A-Train shoves Hughie aside to see the creature, the glass front of the tank mysteriously vanishes, allowing Timothy to escape. The octopus seems to thank Hughie before sliding away.

The incident frightens the Stillwells and embarrasses them. Once they return home, Stan Edgar furiously punishes Hughie by locking him in his cupboard without meals. Hughie reflects on his lonely life, his vague memories of a green flash connected to his parents’ deaths, and the odd strangers who sometimes seem to recognize him.

## Part 3

Hughie spends the summer after the zoo incident trying to avoid A-Train and looking forward to attending a different school. While A-Train prepares for his private academy, the Stillwells plan to send Hughie to Stonewall High in dyed hand-me-down clothes.

One morning, a mysterious letter arrives addressed specifically to Hughie in his cupboard under the stairs. Stan Edgar and Madelyn Stillwell react with panic and refuse to let him read it. They move Hughie into A-Train’s second bedroom, but more letters arrive addressed to him there, proving that whoever is sending them knows exactly where he is.

The letters keep increasing despite Edgar’s efforts to stop them. He blocks the mail slot, boards up the doors, and eventually flees with the family, first to a hotel and then to a remote shack on a rock in the sea. Even there, on the night of Hughie’s eleventh birthday, someone comes knocking at the door.

The visitor is Mother’s Milk, a huge and intimidating man who introduces himself as Mallory’s trusted operative and keeper of the old anti-Vought files. He gives Hughie a birthday cake and finally delivers the letter: Hughie has been accepted to Godolkin, the elite school where young supes learn to control their powers. Hughie is stunned to learn that he is a supe.

Mother’s Milk angrily reveals that the Stillwells have hidden the truth from Hughie for years. Hughie’s parents, Becca and Billy Butcher, were not killed in a car crash as he was told; they were murdered by Homelander. Homelander also tried to kill baby Hughie, but the attack failed, leaving Hughie with his lightning-shaped scar and making him famous in the supe world.

Hughie struggles to believe he could be a supe, but Mother’s Milk points out the strange things that have happened around him whenever he was frightened or angry. Stan Edgar still insists Hughie will not attend Godolkin, but Mother’s Milk refuses to let him interfere. After Edgar insults Grace Mallory, Mother’s Milk loses his temper and triggers a burst of Vought tech that gives A-Train a pig’s tail. Mother’s Milk then prepares to take Hughie to buy his school supplies the next day.

## Part 4

Hughie wakes in the hut hoping Mother’s Milk’s arrival was not a dream, and discovers that it is real when a drone delivers the newspaper. Mother’s Milk explains that they must go to London to buy Hughie’s school supplies and first visit Vought’s private financial office, where Hughie’s parents left him money.

They travel back to the mainland and then to London, where Mother’s Milk leads Hughie to a hidden supe bar. Inside, Hughie is astonished when heroes, handlers, and Vought employees recognize him and treat him as famous. He also meets The Deep, who will teach Defense Against Supe Threats at Godolkin. Through a concealed entrance behind the pub, Mother’s Milk introduces Hughie to a secret Vought supply district.

In the district, Hughie sees supe shops for the first time. At Vought Financial, cold accountants take Hughie and Mother’s Milk deep underground. Hughie discovers an account full of money left by his parents, and Mother’s Milk retrieves a mysterious package from vault 713 on Mallory’s behalf.

Hughie then buys his Godolkin supplies. While being fitted for uniforms, he meets a pale, arrogant boy who boasts about famous supe families, The Seven, and Vought’s best training tracks, making Hughie uncomfortable. Mother’s Milk explains supe rankings and the Godolkin houses, warning that many dangerous heroes came from Vought’s most ambitious circles. Hughie buys books, training gear, a reinforced case, scales, a telescope, notebooks, pens, and ink. As a birthday present, Mother’s Milk buys him a sleek surveillance owl-drone named Terror.

Finally, Hughie visits a specialist to get his power-channeling gear. After trying several devices, he is chosen by a holly-handled focus keyed to a rare Compound V signature. The specialist reveals that the V sample in Hughie’s gear came from the same experimental batch as the serum tied to Homelander, the one that gave Hughie his scar. This unsettles Hughie.

At the end of the day, Mother’s Milk takes Hughie to Paddington for his train back to the Stillwells. Hughie feels overwhelmed because everyone expects great things from him, though he knows almost nothing about supes or his own past. Mother’s Milk reassures him that he will learn at Godolkin and gives him his ticket for September first.

## Part 5

Hughie spends his final days before Godolkin isolated at the Stillwells’ house, reading his schoolbooks and counting down to September first with his owl-drone, Terror. The Stillwells reluctantly take him to King’s Cross, where Stan Edgar mocks the idea of Platform Seven and Three-Quarters and abandons him between platforms seven and eight.

Confused and close to panic, Hughie notices a group of young supes discussing ordinary people and follows them. Annie January kindly explains how to reach the hidden platform: he must walk straight through the barrier. Hughie does so and finds the Godolkin Express waiting.

On the platform, Hughie sees students, families, pets, drones, and trunks everywhere. He boards the train with help from Frenchie and Kimiko, who recognize him by his lightning-shaped scar. From his compartment, Hughie watches Annie’s group say goodbye. He learns that Supersonic is a prefect, Annie is starting at Godolkin too, and Queen Maeve has come to see them off. As the train leaves, the group waves goodbye and Hughie begins his journey to Godolkin.

## Part 6

Hughie boards the Godolkin Express and feels excited to be leaving his old life behind. He is joined by Annie January, who introduces herself after Frenchie and Kimiko stop by. Annie is amazed to learn that Hughie is really Hughie Campbell and asks about his famous lightning scar and the attack by Homelander, though Hughie remembers only a green light.

Hughie and Annie talk about their families. Annie explains that she comes from a religious supe-pageant background and feels pressure to live up to everyone’s expectations, while Hughie describes his unhappy life with the Stillwells and how little he knew about supes before Mother’s Milk found him. Their conversation helps them bond.

When the food cart arrives, Hughie buys a large variety of supe-branded sweets and shares them with Annie, who has only brought unwanted homemade sandwiches. Hughie discovers collectible Vought cards of famous heroes, moving promotional photos, and strange novelty snacks. He learns about Grace Mallory and starts collecting cards while he and Annie sample bizarre every-flavor candies.

Later, The Deep enters looking for his lost octopus, Timothy. Annie then tries to show Hughie a trick to make a tired old rat turn yellow, but it fails. The Deep returns with Victoria Neuman, a confident and talkative new student who says she has already practiced power control and learned her textbooks by heart. She introduces herself and recognizes Hughie from books about supe history.

## Part 7

Victoria is surprised that Hughie does not already know more about Godolkin houses and says she hopes to be sorted into The Seven’s track, though Payback’s track would not be too bad. She leaves with The Deep to continue looking for Timothy, reminding Hughie and Annie to change because they will arrive soon. Annie, annoyed by Victoria and by a failed trick from Frenchie, says she hopes Victoria is not in her house. She explains that everyone expects her to land in the heroic track and worries about disappointing them, especially if she were sorted into Vought Black-Ops.
