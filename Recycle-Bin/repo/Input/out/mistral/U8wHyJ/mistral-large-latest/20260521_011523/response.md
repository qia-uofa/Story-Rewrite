## Goal
Identify a natural pause point in the narrative of the given Harry Potter text to determine the line number `n` where the first part should end. The pause should feel organic, marking a clear transition in the story while ensuring the reader has a satisfying stopping point.

## Design Decisions
- Look for scene transitions, chapter endings, or moments where a major event concludes.
- Avoid cutting off mid-dialogue or mid-action unless it’s a cliffhanger that naturally fits the story’s pacing.
- Prioritize moments where the narrative shifts in tone, setting, or focus (e.g., from action to reflection, from one character’s perspective to another, or from one plot thread to another).
- Ensure the chosen line number is not greater than 500, as per the constraints.

## Potential Pitfalls
- Cutting off too early might leave the reader without enough context or resolution.
- Cutting off too late might exceed the line limit or disrupt the narrative flow.
- Misidentifying a minor pause as a major transition point.

## Approach
1. Scan the text for major transitions or shifts in the story.
2. Note the line numbers where these transitions occur.
3. Evaluate whether the transition provides a natural pause point.
4. Select the most appropriate line number, ensuring it is ≤ 500.
