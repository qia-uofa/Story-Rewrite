Chapter Two shows Hughie Campbell nearly ten years after being left with the Edgar-Stillwell household. He lives miserably in a cupboard under the stairs, ignored or mistreated by Stan Edgar, Madelyn Stillwell, and their spoiled son, A-Train. A-Train receives attention, gifts, and early performance training, while Hughie is treated as an inconvenience and a risk.

On A-Train’s birthday, Hughie is unexpectedly taken along to a research zoo because no one is available to watch him. During the outing, Hughie reflects on the strange things that have happened around him before, such as his hair and skin healing too quickly after injuries and somehow escaping dangerous or frightening situations through impossible bursts of movement or force.

In the reptile house, Hughie discovers he can communicate with an alien boa-like serpent held in a containment enclosure. After A-Train pushes him aside, the containment glass mysteriously destabilizes and vanishes from its frame, allowing the serpent to escape. The serpent appears to thank Hughie before leaving.

Back home, the incident terrifies and enrages Stan Edgar. Rather than admit that Hughie may be enhanced, Edgar punishes him by locking him in the cupboard. As Hughie lies awake, he thinks about his parents, the strange flashes and memories he cannot explain, and the odd people who sometimes seem to recognize him before disappearing.
