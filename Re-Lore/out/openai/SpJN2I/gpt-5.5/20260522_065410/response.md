## Goal
Rewrite the first source summary using the replacement lore: Hughie Campbell, the Edgar-Stillwell household, the hidden superhuman defense world, Mallory, Maeve, Butcher, and Homelander.

## Approach
Keep the same narrative function as the original chapter summary: introduce the hostile ordinary household, show strange signs from the hidden world, explain the fall/disappearance of the great villain, and end with the famous orphan being left with unsuitable guardians.
</file>
<file path="./resop-1.txt">
Chapter One introduces the Edgar-Stillwell household, a proudly respectable and aggressively ordinary family living at number four, Privet Drive. Stan Edgar and Madelyn Stillwell dislike anything that might draw attention from the superhuman world and avoid speaking honestly about Madelyn’s estranged sister, Daphne Campbell, her husband Hugh Campbell Sr., and their infant son, Hughie.

On an otherwise normal day, Stan notices unsettling signs that something classified is happening: drones moving strangely in daylight, unusually dressed operatives gathering in public, unexplained lights in the sky, and whispers about the Campbells, Homelander, and a surviving child named Hughie.

That night, Grace Mallory arrives on Privet Drive and meets Queen Maeve, who has been watching the house. They discuss the news that Homelander, a feared rogue Viltrumite-aligned superhuman, has disappeared after murdering Daphne and Hugh Campbell Sr. but failing to kill their infant son. Hughie survived the attack with a lightning-shaped energy scar, making him famous throughout the hidden superhuman defense world.

Billy Butcher arrives on a flying motorcycle retrofitted with alien anti-gravity technology, carrying baby Hughie. Maeve worries that Edgar and Stillwell are unsuitable guardians, but Mallory insists Hughie should grow up away from fame until he is old enough to understand his past. Mallory leaves Hughie on the doorstep with a letter explaining what happened. As the defense world quietly celebrates Homelander’s disappearance and honors Hughie as the child who survived, baby Hughie sleeps, unaware of his fame or the difficult life awaiting him.
</file>

## Goal
Rewrite the second source summary around Hughie’s childhood neglect and the research-zoo incident.

## Approach
Preserve the original structure: miserable life with guardians, strange uncontrolled incidents, a birthday outing, communication with a serpent, vanishing containment glass, punishment, and Hughie’s confusion about his past.
</file>
<file path="./resop-2.txt">
Chapter Two shows Hughie Campbell nearly ten years after being left with the Edgar-Stillwell household. He lives miserably in a cupboard under the stairs, ignored or mistreated by Stan Edgar, Madelyn Stillwell, and their spoiled son, A-Train. A-Train receives attention, gifts, and early performance training, while Hughie is treated as an inconvenience and a risk.

On A-Train’s birthday, Hughie is unexpectedly taken along to a research zoo because no one is available to watch him. During the outing, Hughie reflects on the strange things that have happened around him before, such as his hair and skin healing too quickly after injuries and somehow escaping dangerous or frightening situations through impossible bursts of movement or force.

In the reptile house, Hughie discovers he can communicate with an alien boa-like serpent held in a containment enclosure. After A-Train pushes him aside, the containment glass mysteriously destabilizes and vanishes from its frame, allowing the serpent to escape. The serpent appears to thank Hughie before leaving.

Back home, the incident terrifies and enrages Stan Edgar. Rather than admit that Hughie may be enhanced, Edgar punishes him by locking him in the cupboard. As Hughie lies awake, he thinks about his parents, the strange flashes and memories he cannot explain, and the odd people who sometimes seem to recognize him before disappearing.
</file>

## Goal
Rewrite the third source summary as Hughie receiving his Guardians Academy summons and learning the truth from Butcher.

## Approach
Follow the same escalation: letters arrive, the guardians try to stop them, the family flees, Butcher arrives at the hut, and Hughie learns about the academy, his powers, his parents, Homelander, and his fame.
</file>
<file path="./resop-3.txt">
Hughie spends the summer after the research-zoo incident being punished and avoiding A-Train and his friends. As the new school year approaches, the Edgar-Stillwell household prepares A-Train for private school and elite athletic testing while planning to send Hughie to Stonewall High in unwanted old clothes.

One morning, Hughie receives his first-ever classified letter, addressed specifically to him in the cupboard under the stairs. Stan Edgar and Madelyn Stillwell react with fear and destroy it before Hughie can read it. More letters arrive, each one correctly tracking Hughie’s location, even after the household moves him into A-Train’s second bedroom. Edgar becomes increasingly desperate, blocking the mail slot, sealing the house, and eventually fleeing with the family to escape the messages.

The letters continue to follow them, first to a hotel and then to a remote hut on a rock in the sea. At midnight on Hughie’s eleventh birthday, Billy Butcher breaks into the hut and gives Hughie a birthday cake and his Guardians Academy acceptance letter. Butcher reveals that Hughie is enhanced and has been accepted into Earth’s classified training institution for young operatives.

Hughie learns that Edgar and Stillwell have hidden the truth from him: his parents did not die in a car crash, but were murdered by Homelander during a covert Viltrumite-linked attack. Homelander also tried to kill Hughie as a baby, but the strike failed, leaving him with a lightning-shaped scar and making him famous throughout the superhuman defense world. Butcher explains that Homelander disappeared after the failed attack.

Edgar tries to stop Hughie from going to the Guardians Academy, but Butcher insists he cannot prevent it. When Edgar insults Grace Mallory, Butcher loses his temper and uses a mutagenic prank-device to give A-Train a pig-like tail. Butcher then tells Hughie they must go to London the next day to buy his academy supplies.
</file>

## Goal
Rewrite the fourth source summary as Hughie’s first guided entry into the hidden superhuman defense world.

## Approach
Map the shopping and bank sequence to the Tailor’s Door, Satellite Five Exchange, GDA Deep Vault, academy supplies, Stormfront, Hedwig, and the Armory. Include the significant gauntlet connection to Homelander’s war harness.
</file>
<file path="./resop-4.txt">
Hughie wakes after Butcher’s arrival and realizes the news about the Guardians Academy was not a dream. Butcher explains that Hughie’s parents left him protected survivor funds in the GDA Deep Vault and takes him to London. Along the way, Hughie learns more about the hidden superhuman defense world, including classified agencies, alien partners, covert transit points, and why so much of Earth’s real defense infrastructure is concealed from ordinary civilians.

They enter the Tailor’s Door, a shabby public house that serves as an access shell for the classified world. Inside, Hughie is recognized and warmly greeted by heroes, retired operatives, alien envoys, and agency staff, including Black Noir, the Guardians Academy instructor for Defensive Tactics Against Alien Threats. Butcher then opens the hidden entrance to the Satellite Five Exchange, a bustling procurement district full of academy supplies, tactical equipment, companion drones, field tools, and alien technology.

At the GDA Deep Vault, Hughie discovers the inheritance left by his parents. Butcher also retrieves a mysterious package from high-security Vault 713 on Grace Mallory’s behalf. Afterward, Hughie buys his academy supplies: uniforms, armored field layers, manuals, med-kits, sensor kits, combat weights, scanners, encrypted tablets, styluses, and field notebooks. During a uniform fitting, he meets Stormfront, a proud and arrogant legacy-enhanced student who boasts about elite hero families, aerial sports, and Dominion Track, making Hughie uncomfortable.

Butcher buys Hughie a snowy owl-shaped courier drone named Hedwig as a birthday present. They finish the day at the Armory, where Hughie is matched with a bonded neural-response gauntlet made from pale composite alloy and a Viltrumite-reactive crystal core. The armorer reveals that the crystal was cut from the same alien strike-fragment as a component in Homelander’s old war harness, making the match unusual and ominous.

As Hughie and Butcher return toward the ordinary world, Hughie feels overwhelmed by his fame and by how much everyone seems to expect from him despite his knowing almost nothing about the defense world. Butcher reassures him that he will learn at the Guardians Academy and gives him his ticket for the journey from King’s Cross on September first.
</file>

## Goal
Rewrite the fifth source summary around Hughie’s departure for the Guardians Academy.

## Approach
Preserve the final days with his guardians, confusion at King’s Cross, discovery of the hidden platform, help from Mother’s Milk’s family, and boarding the Guardians Express.
</file>
<file path="./resop-5.txt">
Hughie spends his last month with the Edgar-Stillwell household mostly ignored by them while he prepares excitedly for the Guardians Academy with his manuals, supplies, bonded gear, and owl-shaped courier drone, Hedwig. On September first, Stan Edgar and Madelyn Stillwell take him to King’s Cross but mock him when there appears to be no Platform Nine and Three-Quarters.

Confused and close to panicking, Hughie overhears a group from the superhuman defense world discussing the same platform. Monique, the warm maternal anchor of Mother’s Milk’s family circle, kindly explains that Hughie must walk straight through the phase-screen barrier between platforms nine and ten. Hughie follows her advice and successfully reaches Platform Nine and Three-Quarters, where the Guardians Express is waiting.

He finds a compartment and gets help from the TNT Twins loading his trunk. The twins recognize Hughie by his lightning-shaped scar, and Hughie overhears the family speaking about him with curiosity, excitement, and sympathy. As the train departs, Monique and the others wave goodbye, and Hughie begins his journey to the Guardians Academy.
</file>

## Goal
Rewrite the sixth source summary as Hughie’s first social experience aboard the Guardians Express.

## Approach
Use Mother’s Milk as the main compartment friend, replace wizarding sweets with hero-world rations and animated mission cards, and introduce Gecko and Starlight in the same role as new classmates with different levels of knowledge and confidence.
</file>
<file path="./resop-6.txt">
Hughie rides the Guardians Express, excited and nervous to leave his old life behind. He shares a compartment with Mother’s Milk, a friendly first-year trainee from a large hero-support family. Mother’s Milk asks about Hughie’s scar and tells him about his relatives, family expectations, older academy contacts, and his worry that he may not live up to the tradition around him.

The two boys bond as Hughie shares the hero-world sweets and novelty rations he buys from the trolley, including Bertie Bott’s Every Flavor Protein Beans and collectible rations containing Animated Mission Cards. Hughie learns about famous heroes, alien battles, and defense strategists from the moving cards, including Grace Mallory.

Gecko comes by looking for his missing bio-engineered toad, and Mother’s Milk talks about academy life, powers, and the pressure of being sorted into a training division. Starlight then arrives with Gecko, shows her confidence and preparation, introduces herself, and reveals that she already knows about Hughie from classified histories and survivor legends.
</file>

## Goal
Rewrite the seventh source summary as a short continuation of the Guardians Express conversation about academy divisions.

## Approach
Replace Hogwarts houses with Guardians Academy divisions and keep the character dynamics: Starlight is knowledgeable and ambitious, Mother’s Milk is irritated and anxious, and Dominion Track carries a troubling reputation.
</file>
<file path="./resop-7.txt">
Starlight tells Hughie and Mother’s Milk that she has been studying the Guardians Academy divisions and hopes to be placed in Guardian Red, though she thinks Horizon Wing would also be respectable. She leaves with Gecko to continue looking for his missing toad.

After she goes, Mother’s Milk complains that Starlight is bossy before admitting that he is worried about his own sorting. His family is strongly associated with Guardian Red, and he fears disappointing them. He is especially anxious about Dominion Track, whose reputation for ambition, authoritarian command theory, and past Viltrumite collaborators makes Hughie uneasy as the train carries them toward the academy.
